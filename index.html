The life work of Virgil Abloh

Virgil Abloh was an American Architect, Artist, and a Fashion Designer. He was one of the most recognizable designer in recent years who has risen to the hights of Fashion and Design. In few years, Virgil went from just selling screen printed Champion clothes, and Ralph Lauren shirts to becoming the first African-American artistic director of the world’s largest fashion house, Louis Vuitton.

Growing up, Virgil was inspired by graffiti, skateboarding, hip-hop, and his biggest inspiration was Michael Jordan. He attended college at the University of Wisconsin-Madison and received a degree in Civil Engineering in 2002.

It was around this time when Virgil brought his screen print design for a t-shirt into his local printing shop to be printed. The design was formatted so perfectly that the shop only needed to hit print, because of his skills, the store offered him a job at the spot. Don C, a colleague of Kanye West, visited a shop looking for a designers where he met Virgil, he introduced Virgil to Kanye shortly after.

Virgil continued his studies and complete his master’s degree in architecture at the Illinois institute of Technology in 2006. After graduating, he was appointed as Kanye’s personal creative director.

Rise to Prominence

In 2009, Kanye and Virgil interned together at Fendi, they were both treated normally like any other interns. This year was the first time the duo attended a fashion show and their interests shifted from spectators on the sideline to competitors on the field.

Virgil’s work as Kanye’s personal creative director, overseeing the stage shows and merchandise. He was the codesigner of Kanye’s “808 & Heartbreak” album cover, he was in charge of the art direction for “My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy”, “Watch the Throne”, and the “Yeezus” tour.

In 2012, Virgil launched his clothing career, Pyrex Vision. He started with screen printing Champion hoodies and gym shorts with the word “Pyrex” and images of renaissance paintings. He then went to buy Ralph Lauren rugby flannels and printed the graphic “Pyrex 23” on the back and sold them for hundreds of dollars. The aim of Pyrex Vision was to represent the importance of youth culture. Pyrex Vision was met with underground acclaim thanks to the help of his friends ASAP Rocky and Kanye. However, for Virgil, this was more of an art experiment rather than a commercial enterprise.

Mainstream Success

In 2013, the idea of merging streetwear and high fashion was the foundation of his next brand, Off-White, which he described as “the gray area between black and white”. When he started the brand, he could not get a writer to write about his brand so he took instagram and made it into his magazine, he created a brand which defies the traditional system of fashion. Soon enough, popular names such as Jay-Z, Beyonce, ASAP Rocky, Kanye West and others were spotted wearing Virgil’s brand. In 2015, Virgil was the only American finalist in LVMH awards which recognized young fashion designers. In just a few years, he took the fashion industry by a storm with Off-White.
